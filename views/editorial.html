Editorial Policy

Swarashtra.in maintains the highest standards of journalistic integrity, accuracy, and independent coverage.

1. Fact-Checking Standards

Accuracy is the core pillar of Swarashtra. We verify all stories, claims, and ground reports extensively before publishing. Our fact-checking standards require:

  • Primary Source Verification: Confirming details with eyewitnesses, direct participants, court filings, government press releases, or official transcripts.
  • Double-Source Policy: Validating controversial or sensitive political claims with at least two independent, reliable sources.
  • Expert Consultations: Engaging domain experts (lawyers, economic analysts, environmentalists, agricultural scientists) to verify technical or legislative data.

2. Sourcing & Attribution

We believe in transparency. All articles must clearly state where information was gathered. Anonymous sources are used only when a source's life, safety, or career is at risk, and the information they provide is crucial to public interest. In such cases, the credibility of the source is vetted by the Editor-in-Chief before publication.

3. Corrections Policy

Despite rigorous edit cycles, errors can happen. When a mistake occurs, we correct it promptly and transparently:

  • Correction Notices: If a major factual change is made, a note is appended to the bottom of the article detailing the date of correction and what was corrected.
  • Typographical Edits: Minor typo corrections that do not alter the facts of the story are updated without a formal notice.
  • Retractions: If a story is found to be fundamentally incorrect, it will be retracted with a full explanation to our readers.

4. Neutral Journalism & Fairness

Swarashtra is non-partisan. We do not support any political party or ideology. We ensure that different perspectives of a debate are presented fairly. Our reporting is distinct from opinion pieces, which are clearly marked as "Opinion" or "Editorial" to prevent reader confusion.

5. Code of Ethics

Our editorial team follows strict ethical guidelines:

  • Conflict of Interest: Journalists must not report on organizations or topics where they have a financial or personal stake.
  • Ethical Gathering: We gather news through open, lawful, and honest methods. Surreptitious recording is prohibited unless vital for public interest investigations.
  • Respect for Privacy: We respect individual privacy, particularly regarding minors or victims of violence, in line with legal frameworks.

6. Sponsored Content Policy

To preserve trust, we draw a clear line between independent journalism and commercial partnerships:

  • Clear Demarcation: Any article funded by an advertiser, sponsor, or corporate partner is explicitly labeled as "Sponsored Content", "Paid Promotion", or "Advertisement".
  • Editorial Independence: Advertisers have no say in the reporting, editing, or selection of our independent, non-sponsored stories.

7. Reader Feedback & Grievances

We welcome reader feedback, critiques, and complaints. If you believe a story is inaccurate or violates journalistic ethics, you can write to our grievance desk. We review all submissions within 48 business hours.

Grievance Desk: editor@swarashtra.in
